My Story So Far

I’ve been running businesses in some shape or form since I was a kid. In middle school, I sold marbles I won in games. In high school, I organized a soccer tournament that almost got me expelled—turns out using the school’s name for marketing without permission comes with consequences. I had to hand over half my earnings just to not get expelled from school. In 2016, I immigrated to the U.S. with exactly $10 in my pocket. That number dropped to zero within minutes when I bought a Dr. Pepper at the airport. At 18, I was dead broke and starting from square 1. But I refused to be just another statistic. I got my GED, then spent a full year working at Amazon, stacking boxes to save up for college. The thing is, I always had this feeling that I was going to do something big; I just didn’t know how or when. So I did what I do best: I started building. I experimented. I learned. I failed. I kept going. Because at my core, I’m a builder. I thrive at the intersection of vision, execution, and storytelling. Follow my journey.
